    Project Overview

    Villi yrttiseikkailu is an Android mobile game available on the Play Store. The game was produced in spring 2022. It is a part of the annual Game Project of Business Information Systems first-year studies. In 2022 the Game Project was a part of the VIRMA (Virtual tourism) -project. The goal was to produce a mobile game for a local business. 

    Our project team’s client was a Tampere-based experience and activity service business Villipihlaja Tmi. Villipihlaja offers a range of services, including guided forest experiences, private cruises, and wild herb courses. The game is based on Villipihlaja’s Wild Vegetable online courses and was developed using Unity with C# language and 2D graphics. 

    Our project team consisted of four students, including two coders and two graphic designers. As the scrum master and one of the graphic designers, my responsibilities included documentation and communication, gameplay planning, designing usability, and creating graphics. Although programming wasn't initially part of my role, I also studied C# during the project.

    Overall, the project was a great learning experience for us, and we're proud of the final product we delivered.

    My Role

  • Scrum Master
  • Graphic Designer
  • UI/UX Designer

    • Problem

    Initially, we had to determine what kind of game we were going to create. For this reason, we had to understand our client's requirements and expectations for the product. Using Unity and C# programming language was a new experience for all of us. As a scrum master, I coordinated meetings with our client throughout the project.
    After our first meeting with the client, we identified some primary challenges that we needed to address:

  • How can we make the game both educational and entertaining?
  • How can we make the game addictive?
  • How can we ensure that the educational content is accurate?
  • How can we create a fresh look and feel that reflects the beauty of Finnish nature?

    • The primary goal of our client was to develop a game that motivates people to learn about nature and enjoy its serene environment.

    Solution

    Scrum Master's View

    As a project team, we tackled the first two problems: How to make an educational game, which is also entertaining and addicting? Initially, we created five different game concepts and then narrowed them down to the best three. We produced simplistic prototypes for these three ideas. We then conducted a Forms survey, where we introduced these prototypes to potential users and collected feedback. The prototypes and feedback were presented to our client, who assisted us in selecting the primary game concept.

    We decided to create an adventure game that takes place in Finnish nature, where the player collects wild herbs. To add an educational aspect to the game, we included a plant book with all the information about wild herbs. By reading about the herbs and learning from their actions in the game, the user can learn about the herbs. We wanted to make the game entertaining for everyone, so we let the users decide how much they want to "study" in the game. To make the game addictive, we added scoring and a time limit. We also made sure that the wild herbs grow in different places each time you play the game. Additionally, the animals in the game will say different things every time you interact with them, which adds to the variability of the game. These features are important for making the game enjoyable and engaging.

    Once our game concept was completed, I was responsible for writing the final game plan with our group as the scrum master. To ensure that our project documentation was of high quality, I used SMART criteria to help define our product backlog and sprint schedules. Additionally, I created a RACI model with our team to ensure that everyone was aware of their responsibilities. As part of the risk management process, we conducted a risk analysis, where we identified all the possible risks we might face and assessed their potential impact on the project. I updated our risk analysis after every sprint and also wrote our new schedules with sprint backlogs while keeping our project documentation up-to-date. To stay on track, I arranged daily meetings with our team and roughly one meeting per sprint with our client.

    Designer's View

    Learning The Wild Herbs

    We ensured that the educational content was accurate through collaboration with our client. I studied their wild herb courses online and identified 21 plants for use in the game. I collected and wrote information about these herbs, which our client reviewed and accepted.

    I also drew accurate wild herbs based on our client's images. It was important that the drawings were recognizable and fit the playful and colorful look of the game.

    Character Development

    As part of our efforts to create an engaging and exciting game, I wanted to give our characters a unique personality. To achieve this, we created three playable characters, each representing a mystical aspect of Finnish nature. These characters were designed to provide players with greater depth in the game. Additionally, we incorporated animals from Finnish nature into the game as guides for players. 

    These characters and animals were illustrated by our other graphic designer. But to make them more lively, I animated them using Unity. Additionally, I wrote individual speeches for each animal, ranging from tips about the game to facts about wild herbs, and even some funny puns.

    The Look and Feel

    To create a finished look and feel of Finnish nature, I added some nature sounds in the background, such as birds singing and bees flying around. In addition, I added some UX sounds, such as clicking sounds when the user is pressing some of the menu icons, ticking clock when the time is running out, and paper sounds when the plant book is being opened.

    I also wanted a finished UI look for the buttons I created. All possible buttons are made with as little text as possible, but so that they are still understandable.

    Creating Finnish Environment

    Once I gained knowledge about the various locations where wild herbs grow, I proceeded to create a map for our game. I drew all the levels along with their objects and decorations. To give it a fresh and authentic look, I selected some traditional Finnish objects and plants for the environment.

    I also created all the other essential views for the game, such as the main menu, the plant book, and the instructions. Everything in the game is drawn using the same color palette that I developed. By connecting these elements with Finnish wild herbs, animals, and sounds, we have created the perfect Finnish environment.

    Wireframes

    Initially, I created the game map as a wireframe which included various areas for the growth of wild herbs that represent Finnish nature. After that, I drew the final bases for each view. The six designated areas are coniferous forest, suburb, village, meadow, park, and deciduous forest.

    Information Architecture

    This is a map I did that depicts the architecture of the application. There are eight different views in the game. When the application is launched, the user is directed to the Main Menu. From there, they can choose to start a new game, read instructions, check information about the game, or exit the application.

    If the user decides to start a new game, they will be able to select their character. During the game, the user can access the plant book, which allows them to open a specific plant and read about it. They can also return to the Main Menu and access the same options as before. The user can resume the game from there.

    Once the timer runs out, the game ends, and the player is shown an end screen. From that screen, the user can view their points, learn more about the wild herbs, and return to the Main Menu.

    Prototypes

    The First Prototype

    We created the first prototype of the game view which showcases the layout we have been working on. Our main objective was to make the joystick button (for moving the character) easily accessible, so we placed it on the bottom left. Similarly, we placed the collect action (the basket) on the bottom right. These two buttons are the primary actions required to play the game. Therefore, we made sure they were big enough to use effortlessly. On the top left, we decided to show the remaining time and the points gathered. On the top right, we placed the plant book.

    In this prototype, you can also see some of the initial versions of the wild herbs and the first playable character. To represent the book and the joystick button, we used placeholders, which we took inspiration from for the final versions.

    The Second Prototype

    In our playtests, we used the second prototype of our game. I made some changes to this version, including replacing the buttons with my own drawings and adding a final base to the level which I drew in Unity. I also added some decorative elements, like lamps, bushes, and benches. 

    Furthermore, I added a home button to the game, which was initially located on the left side and had a different appearance than the other buttons. The points and time are still displayed without any background.

    The Book Prototype

    I also designed the plant book, which served as a background for some of our views in the game. Unfortunately, the plant book prototype was too small to accommodate all the content we wanted to include. Additionally, it had some usability issues, such as the X-button being the same color as the pages.

    The wild herb prototype, which was featured in the book, was intended to be edible Goutweed. However, during a meeting with our client, I discovered that it could be easily mistaken for the extremely poisonous Cowbane. I suggested changing the drawing, but after discussing the matter with our client, they decided to keep it as is and rename it to Cowbane.

    Usability Testing

    Our team conducted a playtest with four different users. We planned three relevant tasks that each test user had to complete. We also created research questions for ourselves to keep track of. Additionally, we wrote questions for the users to answer after the testing ended. One of our team members took notes during the testing process while I led the testing situation. I also interviewed the test users after they had tried the game. During the test, we refrained from giving any instructions to the testers or answering any of their questions.

    As a result of the testing, we identified three main issues:

    1. The plant book did not provide enough clear information about the bad plants.
    2. The users didn't receive sufficient feedback after they picked up wild herbs.
    3. Navigation through different views was unclear.

    1. The Plant Book Information Issue

    A Warning Button

    After testing, I added two new icons to the plant book.
    The first icon is a red warning button which, when clicked, opens a text box that informs the user that collecting the plant will result in the loss of points. It is worth noting that these plants are not meant to be used in real life either.

    An Info Button

    The second icon is an info button which is used to indicate any usage restrictions of a wild herb in real life. When the user clicks on this button, a text box appears with details regarding the specific restrictions of the herb. These plants may be used in real life but with certain restrictions. The player will get points after collecting them.

    2. The Scoring Feedback Issue

    After the user picks up a plant, the plant's name will appear on the screen. If the plant is unhealthy, the user will see a negative number in red color appear on the screen. Their character will also appear sad or sick. Additionally, a corresponding sound effect will be played. On the other hand, if the plant is healthy, a green number will appear on the screen.

    3. The Navigation Issue

    To improve navigation, I added paths leading to the connection points. These paths are designed to help you easily move around the area without getting lost. Secondly, I created signposts in strategic locations to provide clear directions and information on which areas are where. With these changes, I believe that the overall navigation experience will be much smoother and more efficient for everyone.

    Final Designs

    The Game View

    The final version of the game contains all the features from the initial prototype and some newly added ones. I created a new Home Menu button and placed it beside the plant book button. Additionally, I ensured that all the user interface buttons have consistent style and background to enhance their visibility. I also changed the joystick button to have a more universal appearance. 

    Furthermore, I used the same color palette for all graphics. The plants now spawn randomly throughout the levels, providing a unique gaming experience every time. Additionally, the animals now communicate with the user and provide helpful tips.

    The Book View

    I made some updates to the final book design. The plant book is now bigger and has a flatter base to accommodate more text. I selected fonts that complement our handmade and natural style. It was crucial to choose a readable font and keep our information concise.

    I also enhanced the buttons with more color to improve their appearance. Additionally, the final design includes the new information buttons on each plant page.

    Product Success

    Villi Yrttiseikkailu won the vote for the best game of our game project course of the year. We received fantastic feedback on the graphics and details. Our client from Villipihlaja was extremely pleased with the project and its outcome. The representative of the VIRMA project praised our team's performance, and our training team's coach congratulated us on our project.

    The game received a lot of positive feedback about its graphics and details from guests at the game fair. Our graphic design expert teacher also mentioned Villi Yrttiseikkailu at the fair. Even Ubisoft's representative announced that he voted for our game.

    The game now has over 100 downloads on the Play Store and has received a review of 4.8 stars out of 5.

    What I Learned?

    Throughout this project, I gained valuable experience in working on an agile project. I honed my communication skills by effectively engaging with both the client and the project team. Additionally, I learned how to organize and lead meetings, as well as create and deliver presentations. I also developed the ability to schedule and prioritize a project’s backlog, and produce essential project documentation.
    Moreover, I acquired new skills in creating and animating 2D graphics using Unity, Adobe Photoshop, and Gimp. I also got to know the fundamentals of the C# language and gained insights into mobile UI/UX design.
